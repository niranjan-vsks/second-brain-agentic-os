# SKILL_BIBLE: LinkedIn Writing (Technical Practitioner)

> Source: distilled from the owner's LinkedIn OS Master Prompt spec (2026-07) + general practitioner-content patterns. Date: 2026-07-07.
> Refine by extracting the owner's actual top-performing posts once metrics accumulate in the operator_os Published Log — replace generic rules with measured ones.

## Core principles

1. **The claim level is the constraint.** Six levels: shipped / piloting / building / concept / insight / commentary. Every sentence must be defensible at the declared level. Inflation is the fastest way to lose technical credibility — the audience includes hiring managers and engineers who will probe.
2. **Sentence two must confirm the hook.** The reader gives the post ~5 seconds after the hook. If line two doesn't pay off the promise, they're gone.
3. **A specific number in the first line materially raises read-through.** "Cut RAG retrieval latency from 900ms to 210ms" beats "Made our RAG faster" every time. Only when honestly available.
4. **One post, one idea.** Threads of loosely related points perform worse than one sharp point with evidence.

## Frameworks

### The practitioner post (default)

1. Hook: the outcome or the tension (1 line, number if honest)
2. Context: what was being built and why (2-3 lines)
3. The meat: the decision, tradeoff, or failure — with specifics (numbers, tool names, exact errors)
4. The lesson: transferable, one paragraph max
5. Close: soft — a question or a plain statement. No "agree?"

### The build-in-public post (claim: building/piloting)

1. What I'm building + honest stage
2. The interesting problem hit this week
3. The approach being tried (no results claims yet)
4. What's next

### The commentary post (claim: commentary/insight)

1. The trend/event, dated
2. Why the common take is incomplete — from the owner's 7 years of AI experience
3. The specific mechanism most people miss
4. What it means for practitioners

## Formatting rules (hard)

- Paragraphs: 1-3 lines. Mobile-first.
- Hashtags: 0-3, end of post only.
- No engagement bait, no "unpopular opinion", no fake vulnerability arcs.
- Length: 120-260 words for practitioner posts; commentary can run shorter.

## Common mistakes

- Writing the hook first and forcing the body to fit it. Write the body, then extract the hook from the strongest sentence.
- Claiming outcomes at "building" stage — that's tomorrow's contradiction audit finding.
- Explaining basics the target audience (senior engineers, hiring managers) already knows.
- Posting frequency over quality: 2 sharp posts/week beat 5 padded ones.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Claim level declared and honest
- [ ] Hook paid off by line 2
- [ ] At least one number, name, or exact phrase (or an honest reason there isn't one)
- [ ] Passes brand_voice.md banned-word scan
- [ ] The owner could defend every sentence in an interview
